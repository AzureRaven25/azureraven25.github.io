Overview

CyberPatriot is the National Youth Cyber Education Program's flagship competition, where teams of students compete to find and fix cybersecurity vulnerabilities in operating system images under a strict time limit. As Cybersecurity Head and Chief of Competitions for the GSMST Computer Science Club, I led our team to three consecutive National Semifinals at the Platinum tier - the highest regular competition tier before the national finals.

Competition Format

Each round, teams receive virtual machine images running a mix of operating systems - typically Ubuntu, Fedora, Windows 10, and Windows Server. The images are intentionally misconfigured with real-world vulnerabilities: unauthorized users, weak passwords, unnecessary services, firewall misconfigurations, malware persistence mechanisms, and policy violations. Teams score points in real time as they identify and remediate each issue within the competition window.

The Platinum tier requires consistently strong scores across multiple qualifying rounds. Reaching the National Semifinals three years in a row meant our team performed at a high level continuously, not just in a single standout round.

Technical Skills Applied

  • Linux system hardening on Ubuntu, Fedora, and Debian - user auditing, service management, firewall configuration with UFW and iptables, SSH hardening, and file permission remediation
  • Windows 10 and Windows Server security - Local Security Policy, Group Policy, user account control, service auditing, and registry hardening
  • Vulnerability identification and triage under time pressure
  • Bash and PowerShell scripting for rapid automated remediation
  • Network analysis using Cisco Packet Tracer and Wireshark
  • Forensics challenges requiring log analysis and artifact recovery

Leadership and Teaching

Beyond competing, I ran the club's weekly cybersecurity training sessions, teaching 30+ students each week on topics including vulnerability identification, exploitation techniques, and defensive mitigation strategies. I developed the curriculum, led hands-on labs, and coached members through practice rounds to prepare for competitions throughout the season.
